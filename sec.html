LUNAR ECLIPSE

Lunar eclipses occur when Earth aligns between the sun and the moon and casts a shadow across the lunar surface. A lunar eclipse only happens during a full moon and can last as long as six hours, according to the European Space Agency (ESA). There are three types of lunar eclipses depending on how the sun, Earth and moon are aligned at the time of the eclipse. 1. Total lunar eclipse 2. Partial lunar eclipse 3. Penumbral lunar eclipse
